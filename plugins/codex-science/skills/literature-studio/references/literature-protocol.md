# Literature protocol

## Paper card

Capture source ID, citation/version, review status, question, design, population/system, method, sample size, comparator, outcomes, effect and uncertainty, limitations, conflicts/funding, data/code availability, direct claim locations, and reproducibility signals. Leave unknown fields unknown.

## Evidence handling

- Treat full primary sources as stronger evidence than abstracts, summaries, or search snippets.
- Record corrections, retractions, version changes, inaccessible supplements, and non-peer-reviewed status.
- Synthesize by claim or evidence lane rather than summarizing papers one by one.
- Keep supporting, conflicting, and null results together.
- Separate author interpretation from direct measurement and source-backed statements from inference.

## Systematic or scoping work

Record databases, complete queries, dates, filters, deduplication, selection reasons, excluded-source counts, and stopping rule. Use an established reporting framework when the review type requires one, but do not claim compliance without completing its checklist.

## Gap claims

A gap requires both positive evidence that the issue matters and a disconfirming search showing it is not already adequately addressed. State feasibility, first falsifiable study, likely failure mode, and kill criterion.

## Completion gate

- Every material claim resolves to a source or experiment event.
- Every source ID resolves to retrievable metadata or a local file.
- Important contradictions and inaccessible evidence are visible.
- Quotations are exact, short, and location-backed.
- The synthesis scope and date boundary are explicit.
